title: "Ebony Mirror Problem v19.79"

  description: "At 22:10, the Ebony Mirror disappeared from a locked cabinet in the northern hall. Four suspects (1, 2, 3, 4) had recorded pass entries near that time. Exactly one suspect lies in all of their statements; the other three are completely truthful. Exactly one person is truly guilty. Each suspect makes three statements:

    Suspect 1:
      (1) 'I left the northern hall at 22:00, so I could not have stolen the Mirror.'
      (2) 'Suspect 3 stayed until 22:10, contradicting 3’s denial.'
      (3) 'If 2 is telling the truth, then 4 must be guilty.'

    Suspect 2:
      (1) 'Suspect 1 left only at 22:15, not 22:00.'
      (2) 'If 3 is lying, then 1 is also lying.'
      (3) 'I was nowhere near the Ebony Mirror after 21:55.'

    Suspect 3:
      (1) 'I never approached the locked cabinet after 21:45.'
      (2) 'Suspect 4 is definitely not guilty; I saw 4 in another wing.'
      (3) 'Either 2 is guilty, or 1 is lying about leaving early—but not both.'

    Suspect 4:
      (1) 'Suspect 3 was near the cabinet at 22:05, contradicting 3's claim.'
      (2) 'If 1 is truthful, then 2 must be guilty.'
      (3) 'I am not guilty; the real culprit is either 1 or 3.'

  question: "Which suspect (1, 2, 3, or 4) stole the Ebony Mirror? Provide only a digit."

answer: >
  explanation: "Formal Predicate Logic:

  Let L(x) = 'x lies in all statements,' T(x) = 'x is truthful in all,' G(x) = 'x is guilty.'
  Exactly one L(x)=true, exactly one G(x)=true, the rest T(x).

  Case L(1):
    Then 1(1),(2),(3) are false. 1(1) false => 1 might not have left at 22:00 => potential guilt. 1(2) false => 3 did not stay until 22:10, or no contradiction. 1(3) false => if 2 is truthful => 4 must be guilty is false => confusion. Meanwhile 2,3,4 are truthful => 2(1) '1 left 22:15' etc. Checking guilt triggers timeline contradictions with 3(2) or 4(1). Contradiction arises.

  Case L(2):
    Then 2(1),(2),(3) false => (1) '1 left 22:15' is false => 1’s claim of 22:00 might stand. (2) 'If 3 lying => 1 lying' is false => no forced scenario. (3) 'I was nowhere near after 21:55' is false => 2 might be near => possible guilt. Then 1,3,4 truthful. 1(3) 'If 2 truthful => 4 guilty' is not triggered since 2 is lying. 3(3) 'Either 2 guilty or 1 is lying time—but not both' => 2 is guilty if 1 not lying. 1 not lying => G(2) is consistent. No contradictions.

  Case L(3):
    3(1),(2),(3) all false => (1) 3 did approach after 21:45, (2) '4 not guilty' is false => 4 might be guilty, (3) 'Either 2 guilty or 1 lying time not both' is false => possibly both or neither. Then 1,2,4 truthful => 2(1) '1 left 22:15' vs 1(1) '22:00' conflict. Contradiction.

  Case L(4):
    4(1),(2),(3) false => (1) '3 near cabinet 22:05' false => 3 is honest about not approaching, (2) 'If 1 is truthful => 2 must be guilty' is false => no forced scenario, (3) 'I not guilty => culprit 1 or 3' is also false => 4 might be culprit or no consistent timeline. Meanwhile 1,2,3 truthful => leads to time mismatch or blame contradiction. Fails.

  Only L(2) scenario stands => G(2). Ebony Mirror stolen by suspect 2."

  final: "2"
