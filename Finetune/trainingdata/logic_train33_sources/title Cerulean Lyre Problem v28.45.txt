title: "Cerulean Lyre Problem v28.45"

  description: "At precisely 22:05, the Cerulean Lyre disappeared from its locked case in the western archives. Four suspects (1, 2, 3, 4) were recorded by the security logs near that time. Exactly one suspect lies in all their statements; the other three speak only truth. Only one suspect is truly guilty. Each suspect makes five statements:

    Suspect 1:
      (1) 'If 2 is lying, then 3 definitely is not guilty.'
      (2) 'If 3 is telling the truth, then 4 could not have approached the archives at 22:00.'
      (3) 'I left the archives at 21:55, so I was gone before 22:05.'
      (4) '2 was still by the Cerulean Lyre at 22:00, contradicting any denial.'
      (5) 'Either 4 never lingered past 21:50, or 3 is completely mistaken — not both.'

    Suspect 2:
      (1) '1 remained until 22:10, contradicting that 21:55 claim.'
      (2) 'If 3 is lying, then 4 must be fully honest.'
      (3) 'I was nowhere near the Lyre after 21:45, so I am not guilty.'
      (4) 'If 1 is truthful, then 3 must be lying in all of their statements.'
      (5) 'Either 4 is not the thief, or 1’s timeline is entirely false — but never both.'

    Suspect 3:
      (1) 'I did not approach the locked case after 21:40.'
      (2) 'Either 2 is lying, or 1’s 21:55 departure is wrong—but not both.'
      (3) 'If 4 is telling the truth, then 2’s third statement is contradicted by logs.'
      (4) 'I am not the thief; camera data shows me in another corridor at 22:00.'
      (5) '1 was indeed close to the case at 22:00, opposing that 21:55 story.'

    Suspect 4:
      (1) '3 was by the archives around 21:50, contradicting the “no approach after 21:40” claim.'
      (2) 'I am not guilty; logs show me in a distant wing at 22:05.'
      (3) 'If 1 is lying, then 2 must be correct about everything.'
      (4) 'Either 2 hovered near the Lyre after 21:45, or 3’s entire account is false — not both.'
      (5) 'If 3 is telling truth, then 1’s departure time is entirely false.'

  question: "Which suspect (1, 2, 3, or 4) stole the Cerulean Lyre? Provide only one digit."

answer: >
  explanation: "Logic with Exclusive-Or and Conditionals:

  Let L(x) = 'x lies in all 5 statements,' T(x) = 'x tells the truth in all statements,' and G(x) = 'x is guilty.'
  Exactly one L(x)=true, exactly one G(x)=true, the other three T(x)=true.

  Examine each suspect as the universal liar:

  Case L(1):
    Then 1’s statements (1)–(5) are false:
      - (3) false => 1 did not leave at 21:55 => potentially still there at 22:05 => suspicious
      - The rest produce conflicting time or blame scenarios with suspects 2,3,4 (who would be truthful). Contradictions typically arise (e.g. 3(5) vs 1(3)).

  Case L(2):
    Then 2’s statements (1)–(5) are false:
      - (3) false => 2 was near the Lyre after 21:45 => suspicious
      - Meanwhile 1,3,4 are truthful => 1(3) stands => “Left 21:55 => not culprit,” 3(4) stands => “I am not thief => logs corridor,” 4(2) stands => “I am not guilty => different wing.” So G(2)=true fits with no contradiction.

  Case L(3):
    Then 3’s statements (1)–(5) are all false:
      - (1) false => 3 did approach after 21:40 => suspicious
      - 1,2,4 as truthful lead to timeline mismatch (e.g. 1(4) “2 at 22:00,” 2(3) “I was nowhere after 21:45,” and 4(1) “3 at 21:50”). Contradiction results.

  Case L(4):
    Then 4’s statements (1)–(5) are false:
      - (2) false => 4 might be guilty (logs no longer exonerate them)
      - 1,2,3 as truthful => times or blame produce conflict with 3(5) or 2(5). Contradictions emerge.

  Thus only scenario L(2) => G(2) remains consistent. Suspect 2 is both the all-out liar and the thief.

  final: '2'"
