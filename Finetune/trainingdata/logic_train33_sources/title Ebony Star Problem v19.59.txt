  description: "At exactly 20:55, the Ebony Star vanished from a locked display in the eastern conservatory. Four individuals (1, 2, 3, 4) had digital pass entries near that time. Only one suspect lies in all of their statements, while the other three speak only truths. Exactly one is actually guilty. Each suspect provides three statements:

    Suspect 1:
      (1) 'I left at 20:45, so I could not have stolen the Ebony Star.'
      (2) 'If 2 is lying, then 3 is guilty.'
      (3) '4 lingered near the display at 20:50, contradicting 4’s own denial.'

    Suspect 2:
      (1) 'Suspect 1 did not leave before 20:55.'
      (2) 'Suspect 4 is not guilty, because I saw 4 in a distant corridor.'
      (3) 'If 3 is lying, then 1’s timeline must be false.'

    Suspect 3:
      (1) 'I never approached the display after 20:40.'
      (2) 'Either 2 is guilty, or 4 is lying — but not both.'
      (3) 'If 1 is truthful, then 2 cannot possibly be innocent.'

    Suspect 4:
      (1) 'I was nowhere near the Ebony Star at 20:50.'
      (2) '3 was definitely in the conservatory at 20:45, so 3’s statement is false.'
      (3) 'If 2 is truthful, then 1 must be guilty.'

  question: "Which suspect (1, 2, 3, or 4) stole the Ebony Star? Return only a single digit."

answer: >
  explanation: "Formal Deduction:
