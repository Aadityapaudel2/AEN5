title: "Amber Mirror Problem v103.47"

  description: "At 22:05, the Amber Mirror vanished from a locked display in the southern archives. Four suspects (1, 2, 3, 4) each had verified pass usage near that time. Exactly one suspect lies in all five of their statements, while the other three speak only the truth. Only one suspect is truly guilty. Each suspect provides five statements:

    Suspect 1:
      (1) 'If 2 is lying, then 3 cannot possibly have stolen the Amber Mirror.'
      (2) 'If 3 is fully honest, then 4 must have been away from the display at 22:00.'
      (3) 'I left this archive at 21:55, so I was gone before 22:05.'
      (4) '2 was spotted near the locked case at 22:00, contradicting 2’s own denial.'
      (5) 'Either 4 never stayed beyond 21:50, or 3’s entire testimony is false — they can’t both be true.'

    Suspect 2:
      (1) '1 actually remained until 22:05, contradicting that 21:55 exit claim.'
      (2) 'If 3 is lying, then 4 is definitely not the thief.'
      (3) 'I was never near the Amber Mirror after 21:50, so I’m not guilty.'
      (4) 'If 1 is telling the truth, then 3 must be lying in every statement.'
      (5) 'Either 4 is innocent, or 1’s declared timeline is false — but not both.'

    Suspect 3:
      (1) 'I did not approach that display after 21:45.'
      (2) 'If 4 is telling truth, then 2’s statement (3) is contradictory.'
      (3) 'Either 1 is lying, or 2 is guilty, but never both.'
      (4) 'I am not the culprit; the logs show me in a different corridor at 22:00.'
      (5) '1 was definitely near the locked case at 22:00, contradicting 1’s 21:55 claim.'

    Suspect 4:
      (1) '3 was actually near the display around 21:55, which conflicts with 3’s denial.'
      (2) 'If 1 is lying, then 2 is absolutely correct in every statement.'
      (3) 'I am not guilty; a camera log places me elsewhere at 22:05.'
      (4) 'Either 2 lingered past 21:50, or 3’s entire story is made up — but not both.'
      (5) 'If 3 is telling the truth, then 1’s 21:55 departure claim is completely false.'

  question: "Which suspect (1, 2, 3, or 4) stole the Amber Mirror? Provide only a single digit."

answer: >
  explanation: "Logical Deduction:

  Let L(x) = 'x lies in all five statements,' T(x) = 'x is fully truthful,' and G(x) = 'x is guilty.'
  Exactly one suspect has L(x)=true and exactly one G(x)=true. The other three are T(x)=true.

  Examine each suspect as universal liar:

  Case L(1):
    1’s statements (1)–(5) are false:
      • (3) false => 1 didn’t leave at 21:55 => might be present at 22:05.
      • Contradictory conditions with times or blame arise with 2(1) or 3(5).
    Yields contradictions.

  Case L(2):
    2’s statements (1)–(5) are false:
      • (1) false => 1 did not stay 22:05 => so 1’s 21:55 might stand.
      • (3) false => 2 was near the Mirror after 21:50 => suspicious.
      • Meanwhile 1,3,4 are truthful => 1(3) “I left 21:55 => not culprit” => G(1)=false, 3(4) “I am not culprit => G(3)=false,” 4(3) “I am not guilty => G(4)=false,” so G(2)=true. No contradiction.

  Case L(3):
    3’s statements (1)–(5) are false:
      • (1) false => 3 did approach after 21:45 => suspicious.
      • Checking 1,2,4 as truthful leads to direct time mismatches especially with 4(1) or 2(3).
    Contradictions result.

  Case L(4):
    4’s statements (1)–(5) are false:
      • (3) false => 4 might be guilty or logs are invalid.
      • 1,2,3 as truthful produce a conflict with e.g. 3(5) or 2(5).
    Contradiction arises.

  Only scenario L(2)=> G(2) remains consistent. Therefore, suspect 2 is the single liar and the true thief.

  final: '2'"
